Projects:

fe-nc-games

This is a full stack project I made as part of Northcoders software engineering bootcamp, a dummy board game reviews website.
On the front end, users will land on the pictured page - a list of board game reviews and some extra information about them. Users can sort by category, order the results and vote on reviews. They can also click on the title of any given review to be taken to another page where they can view comments and add their own comments. It doesn't have a login feature however, so it's hardcoded to treat all users as "jessjelly" for interactions that require one.
The backend is just a standard API. A handful of CRUD endpoints that act as a middleman between the user and the database.

reMOTe

This is a full stack group project, also made as part of Northcoders software engineering bootcamp. Unlike the other project with Northcoders, we had to come up with the idea, plans and tickets ourselves. We ended up creating an app for users to find and book car services from technicians in their.
The front end has a mobile friendly and accessible interface, allowing users to quick search for a range of services or make their own refined search. They can also sort and order the resulting list of technicians. When a user has decided on a technician, they can tap or click them to book one of their services. Users can also leave reviews for a technicians they have booked services from. Users can also sign up as a technician and offer their own services and review other users.

Minesweeper

This is a Minesweeper clone created in React. It features a timer and counters for revealed and flagged cells, as well as a game size option and a quick reset button. It's also responsive on mobile, with cells also being able to be revealed and flagged with touches instead of clicks.
